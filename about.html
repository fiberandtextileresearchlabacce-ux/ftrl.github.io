About Fiber & Textile Research Laboratory

Fiber & Textile Research Laboratory (FTRL) is a multidisciplinary research facility at Islamic University, Bangladesh, dedicated to advancing scientific innovation in fiber science, textile engineering, nanotechnology, biomaterials, and sustainable materials development. The laboratory integrates concepts from chemistry, materials science, biotechnology, and microbiology to explore next-generation solutions for emerging technological challenges.

The research activities of FTRL encompass the synthesis and characterization of advanced nanomaterials, functional fiber development, smart textiles, antimicrobial materials, biomaterial applications, and sustainable textile technologies. The laboratory also focuses on microbiological investigations and bioactive material studies to develop innovative solutions for healthcare, environmental, and industrial applications.

Through the integration of nanoscience, chemical engineering, biological approaches, and textile technology, FTRL provides a unique platform for interdisciplinary research and innovation. The laboratory emphasizes advanced characterization, experimental investigation, and the development of functional materials with practical applications.

FTRL has become an emerging research platform of Islamic University, contributing to the enhancement of the university's research profile through dedicated scientific activities, student engagement, publications, and collaborative research initiatives. The laboratory continues to encourage young researchers to pursue innovative ideas and contribute meaningful discoveries in the fields of advanced materials, nanotechnology, microbiology, and textile science.
